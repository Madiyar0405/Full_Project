Films

A film – also called a movie, motion picture, moving picture, picture, photoplay or (slang) flick – is a work of visual art that simulates experiences and otherwise communicates ideas, stories, perceptions, feelings, beauty, or atmosphere through the use of moving images. These images are generally accompanied by sound and, more rarely, other sensory stimulations.[1] The word "cinema", short for cinematography, is often used to refer to filmmaking and the film industry, and the art form that is the result of it.

Horror

Horror or horror is a genre of fiction that is intended to terrify, frighten, shock or disgust its readers or viewers by evoking feelings of horror and shock. Literary historian J. A. Cuddon[en] defined horror as "a piece of fiction in prose of variable length ... that shocks or even frightens the reader, or perhaps evokes feelings of disgust or antipathy."[1] Authors of works in the horror genre create an eerie and frightening atmosphere. The horror is often supernatural, although it may not be supernatural. Often the main threat of a work in the "horror" genre can be interpreted as a metaphor for society's great fears: zombie films are fear of cannibalism, werewolves are fear of themselves, vampires are fear of necrophilia, slashers are fear of serial killers, and so on.

Comedy

Comedy is a genre of fiction that consists of discourses or works intended to be humorous or amusing by inducing laughter, especially in theatre, film, stand-up comedy, television, radio, books, or any other entertainment medium. The term originated in ancient Greece: In Athenian democracy, the public opinion of voters was influenced by political satire performed by comic poets in theaters.[1]

Thriller

Thriller is a genre of literature and cinema that aims to evoke in the viewer or reader feelings of anxious anticipation ("suspense"), excitement or fear. The genre has no clear boundaries, elements of thriller are present in many works of other genres. In cinema, thrillers often include detective and spy films, horror films, adventure films, and action films[1].
